PhD Application Checklist: Everything You Need Before Applying

Applying for a PhD can feel overwhelming. There are universities to research, professors to contact, documents to prepare, and deadlines to track. This guide provides a practical roadmap that can help you stay organized throughout the application process.

1. Identify Your Research Interests

Before searching for universities, spend time understanding your research interests. Ask yourself:

  • Which topics genuinely excite you?
  • What research problems would you like to work on?
  • Which projects, internships or thesis work did you enjoy most?

Having a clear direction makes every later step easier.

2. Research Universities and Professors

Many students begin this process several months before applications open. A common period is between March and May, although timelines vary greatly between universities and countries.

Create a list of universities and potential supervisors. Research:

  • Faculty profiles
  • Recent publications
  • Research groups
  • Funding availability
  • Program structure

Try to build a list of at least 20–30 programs. You may rank them based on:

  • Research fit
  • Faculty interests
  • Funding
  • Location
  • QS Rankings
  • Career goals

GradOS Notes can be used to organize and rank your university list.

3. Send Cold Emails To Professors

For many PhD applicants, contacting professors before applying can be helpful. A good email can help you learn:

  • Whether the professor is accepting students
  • Whether funding may be available
  • Whether your profile is a good fit
  • Whether there are upcoming opportunities

If you receive a positive response, keep a record of the conversation. After formally applying, you may send a short update informing the professor that your application has been submitted.

For more details, see our upcoming Cold Email Guide.

4. Prepare Your Documents

Do not wait until deadlines are near. Prepare these documents early.

Curriculum Vitae (CV)

  • Education
  • Research Experience
  • Projects
  • Technical Skills
  • Publications
  • Awards

Statement of Purpose (SOP)

  • Academic background
  • Research interests
  • Why this program
  • Career goals

Motivation Letter

Some universities require a motivation letter instead of an SOP. Prepare drafts early and revise them multiple times.

Research Experience Summary

Maintain short descriptions of your:

  • Internships
  • Projects
  • Thesis work
  • Publications
  • Conference presentations

5. Recommendation Letters

Strong recommendation letters can significantly improve your application. Identify referees early. Potential referees include:

  • Research supervisors
  • Thesis advisors
  • Professors familiar with your work

Provide them with:

  • Your CV
  • SOP Draft
  • Research Interests
  • Application List
  • Submission Deadlines

For more information, see our Recommendation Letter Guide.

6. Track Application Deadlines

Many PhD applications open between June and January, although this varies by university. Track:

  • Application opening dates
  • Application deadlines
  • Funding deadlines
  • Interview dates
  • Recommendation deadlines

GradOS was specifically designed to help manage this process.

7. Submit Applications

✓ CV Updated

✓ SOP Finalized

✓ Motivation Letter Prepared

✓ Transcripts Uploaded

✓ Test Scores Submitted

✓ Recommendation Requests Sent

✓ Research Interests Verified

✓ Funding Opportunities Reviewed

8. Follow Up

If a professor responded positively before submission, you may send a short and polite update after applying. Keep communication professional and concise. Avoid excessive follow-up emails.

Final Thoughts

PhD applications are not a sprint. They often require several months of preparation. Start early. Stay organized. Keep records of universities, professors, deadlines, recommendation letters, and application documents. Most importantly, focus on research fit rather than rankings alone. A well-organized application process can significantly reduce stress and improve your chances of success.
